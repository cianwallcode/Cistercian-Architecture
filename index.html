History of the Order

1098: The Foundation

St. Robert founds the Cistercian Order at Cîteaux, seeking a return to the literal observance of the Rule of St. Benedict.

1112: The Bernardian Era

St. Bernard of Clairvaux joins the order, fueling massive expansion across Europe through his charismatic leadership and architectural influence.

1115: Early Expansion

Foundation of the first four daughter houses: La Ferté, Pontigny, Clairvaux, and Morimond.

1664: The Trappist Reform

Abbot Armand-Jean le Bouthillier de Rancé initiates a return to stricter observance at La Trappe Abbey, distinguishing the Trappist tradition.

1892: Order Formation

The Order of Cistercians of the Strict Observance (O.C.S.O.) is formally established as a distinct religious order.
