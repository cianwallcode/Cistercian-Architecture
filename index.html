Cistercian Architectural Details

Cistercian architecture is defined by an uncompromising simplicity. Abandoning the Romanesque ornamentation, it utilized the pointed arch to permit higher, thinner walls, facilitating light—a metaphor for the divine—without the distraction of complex figurative sculpture.
